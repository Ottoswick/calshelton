Projects I'm working on

  • Getting more homes bult in London by obtaining planning permission for new properties on under-utilised land through my company, Red Banksia. We use the latest technology to source, design and build energy-efficient homes where they are needed most. They also happen to be beautiful to look at.
  • Sourcing commercial property conversion projects for investor and developer partners that are looking for abnormal returns. What are abnormal returns you might ask? Well, the starting point is for 20% of the GDV to be retained as Profit. Contact me if you want to know more.
  • Continuing to build my own portfolio, of course. I am currently acquiring properties for charities and government agencies that will take on a long-term lease. This is for two main reasons; to get a commercial valuation (rather than residential) and also to minimise the asset management. But also, the feel good factor.
  • The biggest barrier to property investing is education. This was true for me and anyone I know that wants to get started on their first deal, either themselves or as an investor on someone elses. I write books on different investing strategies, giving readers everything they need to implement the investment proposal and provide further resources for education or support. I use these books as a personal tool to cement in my mind how things are done. I give examples of what I have done to show what works and what doesn't.

Useful stuff

Below are a few of the things I have found that help me find, assess and manage my properties

  • If you haven't already yet, check out Nimbus Maps and PropertyData to help you analyse an opportunity and be confident in your numbers. I use these platforms almost daily.
  • My return on investment calculator is handy for analysing a BTL or HMO opportunity.
  • Want to make sure you give your tenant the correct documents before they move in? I've got you with my BTL checklist.
  • I like to reduce my admin where possible and I want to know that my rent is going to be paid on time. I stumbled across GoCardless and now ask all my tenants to sign-up for direct debits using their platform.
